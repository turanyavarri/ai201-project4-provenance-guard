# Provenance Guard — Planning Document

## Architecture

### Submission Flow
POST /submit
    → Signal 1 (Groq LLM score)
    → Signal 2 (Stylometric heuristics score)
    → Confidence scoring (combine both signals)
    → Transparency label (based on confidence score)
    → Audit log (write entry)
    → Return response (content_id, attribution, confidence, label)

### Appeal Flow
POST /appeal
    → Look up content_id in storage
    → Update status to "under review"
    → Log appeal + reasoning
    → Return confirmation

### Narrative
A submitted piece of text passes through two independent detection signals — one
semantic (Groq LLM), one structural (stylometric heuristics). Their scores are
combined into a single confidence score, which maps to a transparency label shown
to the reader. Every decision is written to a structured audit log. If a creator
disputes the result, they submit an appeal with their reasoning; the system updates
the content status and logs the appeal alongside the original decision.

## Detection Signals

### Signal 1 — Groq LLM Classification
- **What it measures:** Whether the text reads as AI or human-generated based on
  semantic patterns, stylistic coherence, and writing naturalness.
- **Output:** A score between 0.0 and 1.0 (0.0 = confident human, 1.0 = confident AI)
- **Blind spot:** May misclassify highly polished human writing (e.g. academic papers,
  formal essays) as AI-generated because of their uniform structure and formal tone.

### Signal 2 — Stylometric Heuristics
- **What it measures:** Statistical properties of the text structure — specifically:
  - Sentence length variance (AI text tends to be more uniform)
  - Type-token ratio (vocabulary diversity — AI text reuses words more)
  - Punctuation density (human writing tends to use more varied punctuation)
- **Output:** A score between 0.0 and 1.0 (0.0 = human-like variability, 1.0 = AI-like uniformity)
- **Blind spot:** May misclassify short texts (under ~100 words) because there isn't
  enough data for the statistics to be meaningful.

### Combining the Signals
- Combined confidence = (Signal 1 × 0.6) + (Signal 2 × 0.4)
- Signal 1 is weighted higher because semantic patterns are more reliable than
  structural ones across different writing styles and genres.

  ## Uncertainty Representation

### Confidence Score Thresholds
| Score Range | Attribution | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 0.0 – 0.39 | likely_human | System is fairly confident this is human-written |
| 0.40 – 0.69 | uncertain | Signals are mixed; system cannot make a confident call |
| 0.70 – 1.0 | likely_ai | System is fairly confident this is AI-generated |

### Design Reasoning
- The uncertain band (0.40–0.69) is intentionally wide to protect creators from
  false positives.
- A score of 0.5 means the two signals are roughly split — the system returns
  "uncertain" rather than guessing.
- The system only labels content as AI-generated when confidence is 0.70 or above,
  reflecting that a false positive (wrongly flagging a human writer) is worse than
  a false negative.

### Calibration Approach
- Signal 1 (Groq) is prompted to return a numeric score, not just a binary verdict,
  so it naturally expresses uncertainty.
- Signal 2 (stylometrics) produces a score based on how far the text's statistical
  properties deviate from typical human writing ranges.
- Both scores are combined before thresholding — the system never forces a binary
  decision from a single signal alone.

  ## Transparency Label Variants

These are the exact strings the system returns in the API response and displays to readers.

### High-Confidence AI
> "Our system has determined that this content was likely generated by an AI tool.
> This label is based on an analysis of writing patterns and style. If you are the
> creator and believe this is incorrect, you can submit an appeal."

### Uncertain
> "Our system was unable to make a confident determination about whether this content
> was written by a human or generated by AI. The signals were mixed. If you are the
> creator and believe this label is inaccurate, you can submit an appeal."

### High-Confidence Human
> "Our system has determined that this content was likely written by a human. This
> label is based on an analysis of writing patterns and style."

### Threshold Mapping
| Confidence Score | Label Variant |
|---|---|
| 0.0 – 0.39 | High-Confidence Human |
| 0.40 – 0.69 | Uncertain |
| 0.70 – 1.0 | High-Confidence AI |

## Appeals Workflow

### Who Can Appeal
Any creator who submitted content can appeal by providing the content_id returned
from the original /submit response and their reasoning.

### What the Creator Provides
- content_id: the unique ID of the submission they are disputing
- creator_reasoning: a plain-language explanation of why they believe the
  classification is wrong

### What the System Does
1. Looks up the content_id in the audit log
2. Updates the content status from "classified" to "under_review"
3. Logs a new audit entry capturing:
   - The original classification and confidence score
   - The creator's reasoning
   - A timestamp of when the appeal was filed
4. Returns a confirmation message to the creator

### What a Human Reviewer Would See
When opening the appeal queue, a reviewer sees:
- The original text that was submitted
- The attribution result and confidence score
- Both individual signal scores
- The creator's reasoning for the appeal
- The timestamp of the original classification and the appeal

### What the System Does NOT Do
- No automated re-classification on appeal
- No guarantee of a human review (out of scope for this project)

## Anticipated Edge Cases

### Edge Case 1: Non-native English speakers
A human writer who is not a native English speaker may write in a more formal,
uniform style that resembles AI output. Signal 1 (Groq) may flag the semantic
patterns as AI-like, and Signal 2 may flag the low sentence variance. This could
produce a false positive even at high confidence. The appeals workflow is the
primary mitigation.

### Edge Case 2: Very short submissions
Texts under ~100 words do not provide enough data for stylometric heuristics to
be meaningful. Sentence length variance and type-token ratio are unreliable at
short lengths. The system may produce an overconfident score based mostly on
Signal 1 alone in these cases.

### Edge Case 3: Lightly edited AI output
A user who generates text with AI and then manually edits it may produce content
that scores in the uncertain range. Neither signal is designed to detect partial
AI involvement — only fully AI-generated vs. fully human-written patterns.

### Edge Case 4: Highly polished human writing
Academic papers, legal writing, or professional essays written by humans may score
high on Signal 2 (low variance, formal vocabulary) and potentially on Signal 1 as
well. The wide uncertain band helps here, but false positives are still possible.
